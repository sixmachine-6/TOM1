WELCOME TO TOM VIRTUAL LIBRARY

The TOM Virtual Lab project represents a groundbreaking initiative, utilizing HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to create an interactive web application dedicated to reinforcing students' comprehension of displacement, velocity, and acceleration analysis within engineering dynamics.

The web app employs analytical solutions as the foundation for simulations, ensuring accuracy and reliability in its dynamic analyses. Although real-time interaction with simulations is currently limited to sample scenarios, students can customize analyses for different mechanisms(RRRR, RRRP, RRRRP) by inputting link lengths.

In the study of mechanisms, a four-bar linkage, also called a four-bar, is the simplest closed-chain movable linkage. It consists of four bodies, called bars or links, connected in a loop by four joints. Generally, the joints are configured so the links move in parallel planes, and the assembly is called a planar four-bar linkage.

It is denoted as RRRP, constructed from four links connected by three revolute joints and one prismatic joint. The slider-crank linkage (RRRP) is one type of arrangement such that one link is a crank, which is then connected to a sliderby a connecting rod.

The "rrrrp chain mechanism" is characterized by its configuration of four revolute pairs and one prismatic pair. In this mechanism, each revolute pair allows rotational movement around a joint, while the prismatic pair permits translational motion along a specified axis. This combination of joints facilitates a diverse range of movements, making the mechanism suitable for various applications such as robotic systems, mechanical linkages, or machinery.
